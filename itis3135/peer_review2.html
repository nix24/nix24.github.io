Project Peer Review 2

This page will be dedicated in giving a in-depth review on a Website created for a client. This website was created by Chris Hanson

Home:

First impressions, the color scheme is a nice red/black combination. The black helps to balance out the intensity of lighter shade of red. Overall it's a solid color scheme. The only critisism I have deals with the theme of the website.The website revolves around glass blowing. Since this is the home page, It's a little confusing that there isn't anything related to it when a user first opens the page. I would include a related image somewhere to keep the theme consistent or even a small sentence detailing what to expect.

Besides that small thing I noticed, this is still a nice home page. the navbar is nice and sleak, and the button that links to the about page is a nice eye-catcher!

About:

Looking at the about page, Everything is structured nicely at first. The page is supposed to have a img, and a body of text describing about why the client is providing this service. The only problem as of now is that the imgs on the site seem to be broken. I have notified hris about this issue and I'm confidently sure he will have these fixed soon. As for now however, I can't ignore the broken img as it takes away from the professionalism of the page.

Contact:

The contact page follows a similar format to the home page. Text is all centered at the middle with everything you need to know. This isn't wrong in the slightest. Although I feel a nitpick could be said about the lack of anything going on the page. I think more styling could go a long way for this page. Nothing complex. Instead of adding everything to the middle, Maybe have the heading formatted at the top with email and phone in a border. The way it looks now is a little compact. Besides that, this page has everything it needs info wise and makes for a solid contact page.

Gallery:

For the Gallery, I cannot say much since the problem I mentioned earlier applies to this page as well. Only now the damage is more serious as any page marketing the client's services won't be shown. As per my suggestion above, once Chris can find a way to restore the images back, this issue wouldn't be a problem but for now, I have to mention it.

Location:

The location page is pretty impressive from someone viewing the site for the first time. A direct location to their services is perfect and Chris implemented it very nicely. I do not have any suggestions, I quite like how the page is as it stands.

Final thoughts:

Overall, this page is actually not bad structure wise. Eevrything you need for a good website is there, It's just the one glaring issue keeping it back from being a site to be shown to th epublic. However because of this, once this one issue gets solved, I can say with confidence that Chris created a nice website and I cannot wait to review it in full once these conflicts are resolved.
