Progressive Metal

Yeah. We're going to get a bit niche with these picks but that's the fun it all. Progressive rock started aroung the late 60s in North America. It aimed to be a remix/blend of two opposite groups to create a sound never heard before. When you combine the aggreive power of a amped Guitar with the melody-oriented, smooth sounding composition of synths/classical instruments You get a nice balance that bursts you with energy. It's a perfect mashup that feels like a narrative being told, It is heavily popular in musical scores for games and movies alike.

Future Bass

Future Bass is a fairly recent genre originating in the 2010s from countries like Uk, australis, USA, and japan. It encompasses all of the bass of electronic and dubstep with a warmer, more melodic rhythm. (Are you sensing a pattern here?) after recent years, future bass has taken the world by storm creating 10s to 100s of sub genres that each takes it's own spin on electronic. If you want to join the never ending sea music euphoria, this is your pick.

Modern Classical

Modern Classical is a more specific pick solely on the fact that it's not as widely used as the other two. That being said, insead of making a huge twist or remix, Modern clssical aims to perfect the art of harmonies and melodies alike, combining past piano eras. It's powerful enough to put in a trance like all of your worries have gone away. Modern classical originates from the 1900s in Western-Europe. It's evolved so many times, in the present day, that two pieces of the genre could sound completely different. Present day classical has started in incorporate faster tempos and more complex rhythms. The future looks brright for this genre as a whole.
