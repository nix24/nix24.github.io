Project Peer Review

This page will be dedicated in giving a in-depth review on a Website created for a client. This website was created by Ethan Kalika

Home:

The home page might not have a vibrant color scheme or anything of the likes, but that isn't always nessecary to make a pleasing experience for the client. The monotome colors aren't strong enough to one-up each other which leads to a professional balance. The intro message clearly shows the name of the service and gives a brief description on what to expect. Overall an amazing page!

About:

The about page takes a personal turn as Ethan describes where the passion of photography comes from. It's a decent size and because of the paragraph structure, it doesn't feel like a daunting task to read. Everything smoothly works with each other. There isn't much more I would ask from the page.

If there's one suggestion I could add, it would be to add a picture of yourself somewhere near the top of the page. It's only a suggestionhowever, I just feel more connections when I can relate the words to a face

Contact Page:

This page really shows off Ethan's talent in design with a nice clean form layout. The form gives a friendly apporach with everything layed out perfectly to set up contac and the newsletter. I do not know if the form works however as when I tested the newsletter with my email, nothing happened.

The design is amazing, it just needs the working javascript to bump it up a few more points for me.

Gallery:

A gallery is th eperfect way to showcase his works. It is presented in a slideshow manner which is a great way to showcase everything without slowing down the page due to tons of images. My suggestions comes in 2 things however. The first is that the images are all different sizes. This causes the scrollbar to act a little unpredictable sometimes breaking the page. If the images were all the same size, this would not be an issue

The second suggestion comes from the speed at which the slideshow moves. I'm only given a few seconds to see the image before the next one zooms right on in. I feel it would be a better experience if I could press a button to switch images instead of it being automatic. Besides that, this is a great concept and I'm imressed by Ethan's work so far!

Pricing:

Finally we reach the pricing page. The page itself doesn't need much work to be good. It has everything it needs with each package name, and the benefits you get for choosing that package. I only have one little nitpick and that is the price location. I think it would be nice to have the prie up front and bigger since that would be the thing most clients look for first. Besides that, the page gets the job done and it gets it done right.

Final thoughts:

Overall, this page has been a delight to review. I love the look of professionalism with nice execution. There were a few styling suggestions I made but even when you disregard those, this website does it's job in a nice and ambitious way. Please check Ethan's website for yourself when you get the chance. You won't be dissapointed.
